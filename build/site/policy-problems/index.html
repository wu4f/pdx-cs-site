Not part of handbook, but temporary place to summarize some larger issues revealed by putting together this handbook:


  • Policy for Ph.D. program support through TAships is unclear, especially balance between supporting existing students and funding first-year students. Most departments have a clearer view of this.
  • We lack a definition of “satisfactory progress” which is needed for GAs and (in possibly different form) would be useful for Ph.D.s. Note: university financial aid has its own definition of satisfactory progress, but this has different goals (and is not too relevant since most grad students are not on financial aid).
  • Ph.D. initial advisory committees are honored more in the breach than the observance. We should either find a way to make them work, or get rid of them and find some other way to give students departmental-level support and overview.
  • RA stipend rates will probably need to be systematized.
  • Grad School is likely to significantly loosen restrictions on Ph.D. programs starting in 2026-27, for example by removing requirements for a proposal defense and replacing all the time limits by a single start-to-finish limit (of 9? years). We should consider whether to take advantage of this to change any of our policies.
